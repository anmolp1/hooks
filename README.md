# Hooks

A curated collection of content hook templates — the opening lines that grab attention and stop the scroll.

## What Are Hooks?

A hook is the first thing your audience sees or hears. It determines whether someone keeps watching your video, reads your post, or scrolls past. A great hook creates curiosity, speaks to a specific pain point, and makes the viewer feel like the content was made for them.

## Good vs Bad Hooks

This repo organizes hooks into two categories:

- **Good hooks** — Specific, emotionally compelling, and curiosity-driven. They use concrete details like timeframes, numbers, and relatable situations to pull the viewer in.
- **Bad hooks** — Generic and vague. They describe the same topics but lack the specificity and emotional pull that makes someone stop scrolling.

Every good hook has a corresponding bad hook to show how specificity transforms an ordinary line into an attention-grabbing opener.

### Example

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| How I grew my [thing] | Over the past [timeframe], I have grown my [thing] from [before] to [after] |
| Common mistakes in [topic] | The [number one/biggest] mistake [specific group] make when trying to [desired outcome] — and what to do instead |

## How to Use

1. Browse [list.md](list.md) for hook templates
2. Replace the `[bracketed placeholders]` with your own specifics
3. Use the bad hooks as a reference for what to avoid

## Contributing

Have a hook template that works well? Open a PR and add it to `list.md`. Make sure to add both the good and bad versions to keep the pattern consistent.
